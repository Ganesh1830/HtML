KABBADI

Information

Kabaddi is a popular traditional sport of India that combines strength, speed, agility, and strategy. It originated in ancient India and has been played for centuries, especially in rural areas. Today, kabaddi is recognized as a professional sport and is played at national and international levels. The Pro Kabaddi League (PKL) has played a major role in making the sport popular among the youth and across the world. Kabaddi is a team sport played between two teams, each consisting of seven players on the field. The game is played on a rectangular court divided into two halves. The main objective of the game is for a player, known as the “raider,” to enter the opponent’s half, tag as many defenders as possible, and return safely to their own half without being tackled. During the raid, the raider must continuously chant the word “kabaddi” to prove that they are not taking a breath. If the raider stops chanting, the raid is considered invalid. Each successful tag gives one point to the raiding team, while the defending team earns points by successfully tackling the raider. Players who are tagged or tackled are temporarily out of the game and can be revived when their team scores points. This rule makes the game highly strategic, as teams must balance offense and defense carefully. The match is usually played in two halves of 20 minutes each, with a short break in between. Kabaddi requires excellent physical fitness, quick reflexes, and strong teamwork. Raiders need speed, flexibility, and clever movements to escape defenders, while defenders must work together using techniques like ankle holds, thigh holds, and chain tackles. Mental strength is equally important, as players must make quick decisions under pressure. Apart from physical benefits, kabaddi also teaches discipline, leadership, and coordination. It is an inexpensive sport that requires minimal equipment, making it accessible to people from all backgrounds. Over the years, kabaddi has grown beyond India and is now played in countries like Iran, South Korea, Japan, and Bangladesh. With increasing global recognition and professional leagues, kabaddi continues to inspire millions and remains a proud symbol of India’s sporting heritage.
